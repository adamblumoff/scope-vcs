## Engineering Taste

- Keep implementations as simple as the scope allows. Less is better than more unless there is a strong reason to add machinery.
- Favor long-term maintainable fixes over local patches. Remove dead code, collapse duplicated logic, and simplify tangled flows while fixing bugs.

- Tests should be useful regression protection, not coverage theater. Prefer the test level that catches real failures quickly, including E2E for important UI, IPC, connection, or workflow behavior.

- For redesigns, be willing to be bold and creative, but keep the product usable and wired to existing behavior unless the user explicitly allows backend changes.
